Geography

The country of Brazil occupies roughly half of South America, bordering the Atlantic Ocean. Brazil covers a total area of 3,287,357 sq mi which includes 3,265,080 sq mi of land and 21,411 sq mi of water. The highest point in Brazil is Pico da Neblina at 9,823 ft. Brazil is bordered by the countries of Argentina, Bolivia, Colombia, Guyana, Paraguay, Peru, Suriname, Uruguay, Venezuela, and French Guiana. Much of the climate is tropical, with the south being relatively temperate. The largest river in Brazil, and the second longest in the world, is the Amazon River.

The Amazon rainforest, located primarely in Brazil, is the worlds largest tropical rainforest in the world, spanning approximately 5.5 million square kilomeeters. It is often reffered to as the "lungs of the Earth" because of its role in producing oxygen and obsorbing carbon dioxide. Covering about 60% of Brazil's land area, this dense and biodiverse ecosystem is home to millions of plants, animals, and microorganisms, many of which are found nowhere else on earth. The Amazon river flows through this region, providing a crucial water source and supporting its unparalleled biodiversity. The forest's geograph is characterized by dense canopies, vast floodplains, and intricate river systems, which play a vital role in regulating the global climate and water cycle.
